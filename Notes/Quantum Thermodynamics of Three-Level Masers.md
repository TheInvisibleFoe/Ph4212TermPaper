# Abstract

Quantum Mechanics and Thermodynamics had, up until the mid 20th century, remained largely separate from one another.

Thermodynamics had largely remained focused on macroscopic systems close to equilibrium and devoid of inherent "dynamics". Quantum Mechanics, on the other hand, focused on the isolated, reversible, and unitary dynamics of microscopic particles.

Historically, the critical bridge between these two topics was constructed by H. E. D. Scovil and E. O. Shulz-DuBois. In a seminal paper, published in PRL in 1959, they demonstrated that **a three-level [[Maser]] when operating under the influence of two thermal reservoirs at different temperatures, functions exactly as a heat engine**. This leads to the conclusion that thermal machines *do not require* macroscopic working fluids to be realized. Single atom systems interacting with quantized light fields and thermal baths can convert the incoherent thermal gradients into coherent and useful work.
